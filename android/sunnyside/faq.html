Sunny Side FAQs

The app uses the Meteorologisk Institute's API as the source of UV indexes. Feel free to report any inaccuracy to them.

The Meteorologisk Institute's API was the only free, reliable source providing current UV index and forecast. Other sources were either unreliable or required a monthly fee. If better data sources pop up, they will be considered, but in the meantime this is the best and only option for Sunny Side.

I have not built Sunny Side as a profit generating project. I intend to keep it free. The downside is that I will have limited time to add new features or maintain it. I will do my best to update Sunny Side if any breaking change is introduced by new Android versions.

Please share your idea by sending an email to sunnyside_help@tobianoapps.com. Just keep in mind that this FREE app is developed and maintained in my spare time so I cannot guarantee that any new feature will actually be implemented.

The short answer is: Sunny Side does NOT track your location but third-parties may.

The long answer is: Sunny Side stores your location on your device only. There is no analytics, ads or any tracking logic implemented in the app. However, the app needs to communicate your location to the Meteorologisk Institute's API to get UV index data back. Please refer to their privacy policy. Also, getting your location either through a location request via GPS or via the Google Places SDK shares some of your data with Google. Please refer to Google's privacy policy center to learn more.
